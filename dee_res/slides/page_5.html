Historical Milestones

The key moments that shaped the birth of artificial intelligence

1950

The Turing Test

Proposed by Alan Turing, this test challenged the definition of intelligence. It suggested a machine could be considered "thinking" if it could hold a conversation indistinguishable from a human's.

1956

Dartmouth Workshop

A group of pioneering scientists gathered for a summer-long workshop, where the term "Artificial Intelligence" was officially coined. This event is widely considered the birth of AI as a formal field of research.

1965

ELIZA Chatbot

Created at MIT, ELIZA was one of the first programs capable of processing natural language. It simulated a psychotherapist and showed the potential for human-computer interaction.

1969

General Problem Solver

This program demonstrated a problem-solving approach that separated strategy from specific problems.

1972

Dendral Expert System

An early "expert system" that could identify unknown organic molecules, showing AI's potential in scientific applications.
