First AI Winter (1974-1980)

Causes:

  • Researchers overpromised on what AI could deliver
  • Computational power needed for complex tasks was unavailable

Impact:

Government agencies in the U.S. and U.K. cut funding for undirected AI research, leading to a significant slowdown in the field.

Second AI Winter (1987-1993)

Causes:

  • Collapse of market for specialized AI hardware
  • Expert systems proved brittle outside narrow expertise

Impact:

Cheaper and more powerful desktop computers began to dominate the market, making specialized AI machines obsolete.

Key Takeaways:

AI Winters were critical reality checks that exposed the limitations of existing approaches

These periods forced the field to mature and develop more rigorous approaches
